  • Research

    High energy pheno is a super broad topic which encompasses studies of models for new physics and how we might observe effects of those models. I work specifically on models for dark sector physics --- physics which interacts weakly with Standard Model photons --- including dark matter and gravitational waves.

    Since the late 1800s, physicists have known that the matter we interact with through the Standard Model does not constitute a majority of the energy in the Universe. In fact, it makes up only about 4% of it. The remaining 96% of the energy is split into dark matter and dark energy, and determining the nature of these dark sectors is a fundamental goal of elementary particle physics. I work on solving 27% of that mystery by developing methods to search for dark matter, a component of matter that interacts weakly (or not at all) with electromagnetism. In particular, I've worked on axion detection using superconducting radiofrequency cavities, potential high- frequency gravitational wave detectors that could search for dark matter signals from primordial black holes or superradiance, and the collider phyisics of scalar singlet dark matter.
